Step-by-Step Guide for Writing an A Paper on the First Great Awakening

Step 1: Understand the Assignment

Carefully read the prompt to focus on the central theme: how the First Great Awakening (1730s–1740s) shaped revolutionary ideas and laid the groundwork for the American Revolution.

Identify the key components:

  • The First Great Awakening as a revolutionary event.
  • Its political, social, and cultural impacts.
  • Themes of individual empowerment, authority challenges, and colonial unity.

Step 2: Conduct Research

  • Gather Sources: Use primary sources like sermons by Jonathan Edwards (Sinners in the Hands of an Angry God) and George Whitefield, and secondary sources such as analyses by historians like Paul Johnson.
  • Take Notes: Organize evidence supporting the Awakening’s influence on political, social, and cultural developments. Note specific examples.
  • Organize Information: Create an outline or chart to align evidence with each of the main impacts (political, social, cultural).

Step 3: Outline the Essay

Note: While the minimum requirement is five paragraphs (introduction, three body paragraphs, and conclusion), A-level work should go beyond this. Include additional body paragraphs or a deeper exploration of evidence to demonstrate exceptional critical thinking and analysis.

  1. Introduction:
    • Start with a hook (e.g., a powerful quote from a sermon or an impactful statistic about the Great Awakening).
    • Provide background: Briefly explain the First Great Awakening and its historical significance.
    • State your thesis: Clearly outline how the Great Awakening influenced revolutionary ideas by fostering individual empowerment, challenging authority, and promoting colonial unity.
  2. Body Paragraphs:
    • Paragraph 1 (Political Impact): Discuss how the Great Awakening empowered individuals to question political authority and embrace liberty and equality.
    • Paragraph 2 (Social Impact): Explain how the revival united diverse groups across colonies, fostering community cohesion.
    • Paragraph 3 (Cultural Impact): Analyze how the Awakening encouraged questioning of traditional authorities, laying the groundwork for revolutionary thinking.
    • Additional Paragraphs (Optional for A-level Work): Explore print culture's role, or how the Awakening influenced specific revolutionary leaders or movements.
  3. Conclusion:
    • Summarize key arguments, reiterating how the Awakening shaped revolutionary thought.
    • End with a forward-looking statement about the enduring impact of the Great Awakening’s principles.

Step 4: Write the Essay

  • Drafting: Write clear, concise paragraphs with strong topic sentences. Use evidence effectively.
  • Integrate Evidence: Introduce sources and explain their relevance, citing properly (MLA or APA).
  • Critical Analysis: Go beyond summarizing; analyze how each point connects to revolutionary ideas and the broader colonial context.

Step 5: Revise and Edit

  • Check Organization: Ensure logical progression of ideas and smooth transitions between paragraphs.
  • Proofread: Correct grammar, punctuation, and spelling errors. Ensure clarity and conciseness.
  • Meet Requirements: Confirm the essay meets the five-paragraph minimum but strive for additional depth to exceed expectations.
  • Polish Language: Use formal academic tone and avoid redundancy or passive voice.

Step 6: Submit with Confidence

Verify that the essay adheres to all guidelines, including formatting and length. Ensure it is submitted on time.

Reminder: While meeting the five-paragraph minimum is acceptable, striving for A-level work requires going beyond the minimum by including more in-depth analysis, additional evidence, and well-developed paragraphs. This demonstrates your engagement with the topic and critical thinking skills.
