Manifest Destiny: Providence, Progress, and the American Mission

1845–1860 | Lecture 1

I. The American Mission

American Progress (John Gast, 1872)

Q: What is being built? Who leads the way?

A National Mission, Not a Myth

  • America as providential republic
  • Destined to extend liberty and order
  • Not conquest — settlement of open land

John L. O’Sullivan (1845)

"Our manifest destiny to overspread the continent allotted by Providence for the free development of our yearly multiplying millions."

— United States Magazine and Democratic Review

II. Foundations of Destiny

1. Anglo-Saxon Institutions

  • English common law
  • Representative government
  • Protestant work ethic
  • Proven superiority in stable republics

2. The Sacred Duty

  • Spread self-government
  • Cultivate the soil
  • Build families, churches, schools

3. The Open Continent

  • Vast unclaimed prairies
  • Treaties with Britain (Oregon), Mexico (Texas, California)
  • Purchase: Louisiana, Gadsden

4. Inevitable Progress

  • Telegraphs, railroads, steamboats
  • From wilderness to farms in a generation
  • Civilization follows the plow

III. Legal & Diplomatic Triumphs

Texas Annexation (1845)

  • Independent republic votes to join U.S.
  • Joint Resolution of Congress
  • Consent of the governed

Oregon Treaty (1846)

  • Peaceful division with Britain
  • "54°40' or Fight!" → compromise at 49°
  • Model of diplomatic maturity

Mexican-American War (1846–48)

  • Disputed Texas border
  • Polk’s offer to purchase California rejected
  • Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo: $15M for 525,000 sq mi

Gadsden Purchase (1853)

  • $10M for southern rail route
  • Voluntary sale by Mexico

V. Critiques from Within

Henry David Thoreau (1846)

"The government is best which governs least."

Opposed federal overreach in Mexican War

John C. Calhoun (1848)

"We have never dreamt of incorporating into our Union any but the Caucasian race."

Feared centralized empire would undermine federalism

Conservative Concern

  • Expansion must not weaken states’ rights
  • Polk’s war = executive overreach
  • Goal: settlement, not imperial rule

VI. Legacy of Manifest Destiny

Continental Nation Achieved

  • From 13 colonies → 3 million sq mi
  • Railroads bind East to West
  • Greatest republic in history

Paul Johnson’s Verdict

"The doctrine of Manifest Destiny was not mere jingoism; it was a recognition of geographical and demographic reality."

— A History of the American People (1997)

Echoes Today

  • American exceptionalism
  • Confidence in self-government
  • Responsibility to lead
