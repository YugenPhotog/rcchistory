## SLIDE 1

# Century of Genocide – Unbearable Consequences  

- Holocaust Studies often focuses on the **Jewish Holocaust** alone.  
- This can create two problematic assumptions:  
  1. The Holocaust was **sui generis** – totally unique and an aberration in history and human nature.  
  2. Other atrocities (especially Communist and some fascist crimes) are minimized or ignored.  
- There was never a **"Nuremberg Trial"** for Communist atrocities:  
  - No systematic punishment of perpetrators.  
  - No global discrediting of radical left-wing ideology.  
- Many left-wing ideologues continued to enjoy respected positions in Western societies and universities even after mass atrocities were known.  

**SPEAKER NOTES:**  
> Clarify that this is not a "whataboutism" to downplay the Holocaust, but a call to see it alongside other 20th‑century horrors. Encourage students to compare *scale, intent, ideology,* and *methods* across cases.

**STUDY BUDDY NOTES:**
> Key concept: The Holocaust wasn't humanity's only mass atrocity. Understanding it requires looking at the broader pattern of 20th-century genocides and the ideologies behind them.

## SLIDE 2

# [SECTION] The Holocaust (Shoah)

## Jewish Holocaust in Modern Europe  

- Long **Jewish Diaspora** comes into conflict with rising **European nationalism**.  
- This tension produces what contemporaries call **"The Jewish Question."**  
  - Hundreds of pamphlets, articles, and books propose "solutions."  
- By the late **19th century**, the Jewish Question takes on an openly **antisemitic** tone.  
- Growing calls for legislation to **"de‑Jewify"**:  
  - The press  
  - Education  
  - Culture  
  - The economy  

**SPEAKER NOTES:**  
> Stress the gradual evolution: social prejudice → political antisemitism → state-sponsored exclusion → genocide. It did not "come out of nowhere" in 1933 or 1941.

**STUDY BUDDY NOTES:**
> The Holocaust had deep historical roots. It evolved from centuries of antisemitism combined with modern nationalism and pseudoscientific racism.

## SLIDE 3

## The "Final Solution" to the Jewish Question  

Key mechanisms of the Nazi program:  

- **Ghettos**  
  - Forced segregation of Jews into walled districts with severe overcrowding and starvation.  

- **Mobile Killing Squads (Einsatzgruppen)**  
  - Followed advancing armies into Eastern Europe.  
  - Conducted mass shootings, especially of Jews, Roma, and political commissars.  

- **Killing Centers (Extermination Camps)**  
  - Purpose-built sites such as Auschwitz-Birkenau, Treblinka, Sobibor, Belzec, Chelmno.  
  - Industrialized mass murder using gas chambers, forced labor to death, starvation, disease.  

**SPEAKER NOTES:**  
> Explain the distinction between **concentration camps**, **labor camps**, and **extermination camps**. If students have read *Night* or watched *Schindler's List*, connect those narratives to this structure.

## SLIDE 7

# [SECTION] Armenian Genocide  

## Background  

- **Ottoman Empire's attempted genocide** of its ethnic Christian Armenian population during and after **World War I**.  
- Armenians viewed with suspicion as a potentially disloyal minority aligned with foreign Christian powers (Russia, Western Europe).  

[IMG_PLACEHOLDER: Map of Ottoman Empire showing Armenian population centers before 1915]

**STUDY BUDDY NOTES:**
> The Armenian Genocide (1915-1923) was the first modern genocide of the 20th century. It became a model for later atrocities.

---

## SLIDE 8

## Stages of Destruction  

- Begins with wholesale killing of **able-bodied Armenian males** through:  
  - Massacres  
  - Forced labor battalions  
- Followed by **mass deportation** of women, children, and the elderly on **death marches**:  
  - Deprived of food and water  
  - Subjected to rape and massacre  

### Methods of Mass Killing  

- Massacres included:  
  - **Burnings**  
  - **Drownings**  
  - Killings by doctors (e.g., poison, medical abuse)  

**SPEAKER NOTES:**  
> Connect to ongoing political debates over naming this as "genocide." Ask why states sometimes resist acknowledging genocides, and what is at stake in the label.

## SLIDE 10

# [SECTION] Communist Atrocities – 20th Century  

## Major Episodes  

- **The Red Terror** (early Bolshevik period).  
- **The Gulag** – extensive system of forced labor camps.  
- **The Great Terror / Great Purge** (Stalin's purges of the 1930s).  
- **Ukrainian Famine (Holodomor)** – man-made famine in early 1930s.  
- **Communist Genocide in Cambodia** under the Khmer Rouge.  

**SPEAKER NOTES:**  
> Stress the difficulty of precise numbers and the range of scholarly estimates. The key point: these are *massive* crimes against humanity, not isolated excesses.

## SLIDE 12

## The Red Terror and the Gulag  

- **Red Terror**: early campaign of arrests, executions, and repression following Bolshevik Revolution.  
- **Gulag**: network of labor camps for political prisoners and "enemies of the people."  
- Prisoners subjected to:  
  - Forced labor in extreme climates.  
  - Starvation, disease, and violence.  

### Example: Pavel Florensky  

- Philosopher and theologian **Pavel Florensky** arrested and sent to the Gulag.  
- After three years, taken to the woods and shot.  

**SPEAKER NOTES:**  
> Use Florensky as a micro‑history: a single person whose fate illustrates systems of repression.

---

## SLIDE 13

## Ukrainian Famine (Holodomor)  

- Artificial famine in **Ukraine** (and other grain regions) linked to forced collectivization and state requisition of grain.  
- Millions die of starvation.  
- Images show:  
  - Emaciated bodies.  
  - Mass graves.  
  - Rural devastation.  

[IMG_PLACEHOLDER: Historical photograph documenting Holodomor victims]

[POPUP: "The term 'Holodomor' combines Ukrainian words for 'hunger' (holod) and 'extermination' (mor)."]

### USSR – Overall Toll  

- Estimated **20 million** people killed in the USSR through:  
  - Purges  
  - Executions  
  - Forced labor  
  - Famine and related policies  

**SPEAKER NOTES:**  
> Ask why famine caused by policy should be classed alongside direct mass killing. Explore the concept of **"politicide"** or destruction of perceived political enemies.
