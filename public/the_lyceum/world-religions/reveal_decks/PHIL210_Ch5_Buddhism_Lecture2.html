Sexual Yoga in Tantric Buddhism

Some tantric texts describe sexual union as representing the non-dual union of wisdom and compassion. This teaching appears in three main ways:

  • Symbolic: imagery expressing non-dual realization
  • Internal yogic practice: visualization and subtle-body meditation
  • Literal ritual (rare): highly restricted advanced practice in specific lineages

In most Tibetan Buddhist contexts, everyday practice focuses on meditation, mantra, and ethical discipline, not sexual rites.

Emphasize that Vajrayana reframes how categories are perceived but does not discard ethics. Transgressive elements exist historically but are controlled, symbolic, and advanced.

Helpful summary line: Tantra breaks attachment to ego and rigid concepts, not moral responsibility.
