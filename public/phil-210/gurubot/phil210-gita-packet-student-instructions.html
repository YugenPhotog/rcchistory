Sacred Writings: The Bhagavad Gita — Reading Packet Instructions

For this unit's sacred text reading, you will work through an interactive online packet containing selected verses from the Bhagavad Gita, comprehension questions, and thought questions that you will type directly into the page. Please read these instructions before you open the packet — a couple of technical details will save you real frustration.

🔗 Open the packet here:
The Bhagavad Gita — Student Reading Packet
⚠️ Important — use a real browser. Open the packet in Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge on a computer, not inside the Canvas mobile app or an in-app preview window. The export buttons and automatic answer-saving only work reliably in a full browser. Do not use private/incognito mode, and complete your work on one device in one browser — your typed answers are saved in that browser, and they will not follow you to a different computer or phone.

Step 1 — Read the packet

Start with the Before You Read page and the two reference lists (People and Essential Terms and Names and Epithets in the Reading). Krishna and Arjuna are each called by several different names in the text — the epithet list will keep you oriented. Then read all nine sections of selected verses. The italicized introduction at the top of each section summarizes what happens in the omitted passages, so read those too; they are part of the assignment.

Step 2 — Complete the Reading Checks

The ten multiple-choice Reading Checks confirm that you read the selection closely. Answer them as you read or after you finish. Every answer comes directly from the assigned verses — if you are unsure, go back to the text rather than guessing.

Step 3 — Type your Thought Question answers

Enter your full name in the Name field, then type your answer to each of the four Thought Questions in the shaded box below it. Each answer should be 4–5 complete sentences and must include at least one parenthetical verse citation, for example (2.47). Your answers save automatically as you type, so you can close the tab and return later — as long as you come back in the same browser on the same device.

Step 4 — Export and submit your work

When your name and all four answers are complete, use the two buttons at the top right of the packet page:

Export Full Packet (PDF) Opens your browser's print window. Set the destination to “Save as PDF” and save the file. This PDF contains the whole packet with your typed answers included. Upload it to this Canvas assignment.
Export Answers (.txt) Downloads a small text file containing only your name, the four Thought Questions, and your typed answers. You will upload this .txt file to GuruBot in Step 5 below.

Step 5 — Converse with GuruBot

GuruBot is an AI teaching assistant whose purpose is to assess your knowledge of the reading and guide you toward wisdom. It will ask you three questions, one at a time: the first checks your understanding of the assigned verses, and the second and third ask you to apply the Gita's teachings to real situations. Answer thoughtfully and in your own words — GuruBot has the entire assigned selection in front of it and will respond to what you actually write, with specific verse citations.

How to complete the GuruBot conversation:
1. Open GuruBot here: GuruBot — Bhagavad Gita Conversation (you may be asked to sign in with a free Claude account)
2. Click Upload .txt and choose the answers file you exported in Step 4, then tell GuruBot your name
3. Answer all three questions in complete sentences — one-line answers will earn you follow-up questions
4. When GuruBot closes the conversation, click Download Transcript at the top of the page
5. Upload the transcript file to this Canvas assignment along with your PDF

GuruBot gives feedback, not grades — your professor grades the assignment. The conversation is part of your submission, so treat it as academic work: honest effort, complete sentences, and your own thinking.

Troubleshooting

The buttons don't do anything. You are probably viewing the packet inside the Canvas app or an in-app preview. Copy the packet link into Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge and try again. My typed answers disappeared. You are likely on a different device or browser than the one you typed them in, or you were working in private/incognito mode. Return to the original browser; if the answers are truly gone, retype them and export immediately. The PDF is missing my answers. Make sure you typed directly into the shaded boxes on the page and that the answers are visible on screen before you click Export Full Packet.

✅ Before you submit, check that you have:
• Read all nine sections, including the section introductions
• Answered all ten Reading Checks
• Typed your full name in the Name field
• Written 4–5 sentences with at least one verse citation for each of the four Thought Questions
• Uploaded the PDF to this assignment
• Uploaded your .txt answers file to GuruBot and completed all three questions
• Uploaded the GuruBot transcript to this assignment
