## 1 · The Opening, Revelation, and the Qur’an

The Qur’an presents itself as guidance, recitation, warning, mercy, and a text whose power is experienced through sound as well as meaning.

1:6

1:7
The path of those whom You have favoured; Not the (path) of those who earn Yours anger nor of those who go astray.

## 2 · God: Oneness, Majesty, and Knowledge

Islamic teaching begins with tawhid, the oneness and uniqueness of God. These passages combine uncompromising monotheism with divine life, knowledge, power, mercy, and transcendence.

Qur’an 112:1–4 · Al-Ikhlas · Sincerity
God is One
This short surah is a classic statement of tawhid.
Focus while reading:
What does the passage affirm, and what forms of divine parentage or comparison does it deny?
112:1
Say: He is God, One,
112:2
God, the One on whom all depend.
112:3
He does not beget, nor was He begotten,
112:4
and there is nothing comparable to Him.

Qur’an 2:255 · Al-Baqarah · The Cow
The Throne Verse
Often memorized and recited for protection, this verse presents God as living, sovereign, knowing, and tireless.
Focus while reading:
Trace the claims about divine life, ownership, knowledge, intercession, and power.
2:255
God—there is no deity except Him, the Living, the Self-Sustaining. Neither drowsiness nor sleep overtakes Him. To Him belongs whatever is in the heavens and whatever is on earth. Who can intercede with Him except by His permission? He knows what lies before them and what lies behind them, while they grasp nothing of His knowledge except what He wills. His throne extends over the heavens and the earth, and preserving them does not weary Him. He is the Most High, the Magnificent.

Qur’an 59:22–24 · Al-Hashr · The Gathering
The divine names
These verses gather names and attributes used in Muslim prayer and theology.
Focus while reading:
Which names emphasize mercy, authority, peace, creativity, or judgment?

59:23

59:24
He is God, the Creator, the Shaper out of nothing, the Fashioner. His are the most beautiful names. All that is in the heavens and the earth glorifies Him, and He is the Mighty, the Wise.

Qur’an 6:101–103 · Al-An‘am · Cattle
Creator beyond human categories
The passage rejects divine offspring and denies that ordinary sight can encompass God.
Focus while reading:

## 3 · Creation, Humanity, and Moral Responsibility

Human beings are created, taught, tested, guided, and made responsible on earth. The Qur’an does not teach inherited guilt from Adam; Adam repents and receives mercy.

Qur’an 2:30–39 · Al-Baqarah · The Cow
Adam, knowledge, temptation, and guidance
The narrative introduces humanity as khalifah, often translated vicegerent, steward, or successive authority on earth.
Focus while reading:
Why do the angels question the human vocation? What distinguishes Adam? What follows the human failure?
2:30
When your Lord said to the angels, ‘I am placing a khalifah on earth,’ they said, ‘Will You place there one who will cause corruption and shed blood, while we glorify You with praise and sanctify You?’ He said, ‘I know what you do not know.’
2:31
And He taught Adam all the names, then showed them to the angels, saying: Inform Me of the names of these, if you are truthful.
2:32
They said: Be glorified! We have no knowledge saving that which You have taught us. Indeed, You, only You, are the Knower, the Wise.
2:33
He said: O Adam! Inform them of their names, and when he had informed them of their names, He said: Did I not tell you that I know the secret of the heavens and the earth? And I know that which you disclose and which you hide.
2:34
And when We said to the angels: Prostrate yourselves before Adam, they fell prostrate, all except Iblis. He demurred through pride, and so became a disbeliever.
2:35
And We said: O Adam! Dwell you and your wife in the Garden, and eat you freely (of the fruits) of it where you will; but come not nigh this tree lest you become wrong-doers.
2:36

2:37

2:38
We said: Go down, all of you, from from here; but truly there comes to you from Me a guidance; and whoso follows My guidance, there shall no fear come upon them neither shall they grieve.
2:39
But they who disbelieve, and deny Our revelations, such are rightful Peoples of the Fire. They will abide in it.
Translation/context note:

## 4 · Prophets and the Abrahamic Story

The Qur’an places Muhammad within a long line of prophets. Abraham, Moses, Mary, and Jesus are not marginal figures; they help define Islamic continuity with and critique of earlier communities.

## 5 · Worship and the Five Pillars

The full Five Pillars are not listed together in one Qur’anic passage, but prayer, almsgiving, fasting, pilgrimage, and confession of God’s oneness and Muhammad’s mission are grounded across the Qur’an and later hadith.

Qur’an 2:43 · Al-Baqarah · The Cow
Prayer and required alms
Prayer and zakat are repeatedly paired.
Focus while reading:
Why might bodily worship and material responsibility belong together?
2:43
Establish worship, pay the required alms, and bow your heads with those who bow (in worship).

Qur’an 2:183–187 · Al-Baqarah · The Cow
The fast of Ramadan
These verses establish fasting, exemptions, the month of revelation, and the daily limits of the fast.
Focus while reading:
What purposes, accommodations, and boundaries appear?
2:183
O you who believe! Fasting is prescribed for you, even as it was prescribed for those before you, that you may ward off (evil);
2:184
(Fast) a certain number of days; and (for) him who is sick among you, or on a journey, (the same) number of other days; and for those who can afford it there is a ransom: the feeding of a man in need - but whoso does good of his own accord, it is better for him: and that you fast is better for you if you did but know -
2:185
The month of Ramadan in which was revealed the Qur'an, a guidance for mankind, and clear proofs of the guidance, and the Criterion (of right and wrong). And whoever of you is present, let him fast the month, and whoever of you is sick or on a journey, (let him fast the same) number of other days. God desires for you ease; He desires not hardship for you; and (He desires) that you should complete the period, and that you should magnify God for having guided you, and that peradventure you may be thankful.
2:186
And when My servants question you concerning Me, then surely I am nigh. I answer the prayer of the suppliant when he cries to Me. So let them hear My call and let them trust in Me, in order that they may be led aright.
2:187
It is made lawful for you to go in to your wives on the night of the fast. They are raiment for you and you are raiment for them. God is Aware that you were deceiving yourselves in this respect and He has turned in mercy toward you and relieved you. Then strictly observe the fast till nightfall and touch them not, but be at your devotions in the mosques. These are the limits imposed by God, so approach them not. Thus God explains His revelation to mankind that they may ward off (evil).

## 6 · Righteousness, Justice, Family, and the Vulnerable

Qur’anic ethics join worship to generosity, truthful covenant, care for family, economic fairness, and public justice. These selections resemble a compact moral code while retaining their own Islamic vocabulary.

Qur’an 2:177 · Al-Baqarah · The Cow
Righteousness is more than ritual direction
The verse combines belief, generosity, worship, promises, and endurance.
Focus while reading:
Why does the verse begin by rejecting a merely external definition of righteousness?
2:177
It is not righteousness that you turn your faces to the East and the West; but righteous is he who believes in God and the Last Day and the angels and the Scripture and the prophets; and gives wealth, for love of Him, to kinsfolk and to orphans and the needy and the wayfarer and to those who ask, and to set slaves free; and observes proper worship and pays the required alms. And those who keep their treaty when they make one, and the patient in tribulation and adversity and time of stress. Such are they who are sincere. Such are the God-fearing.

Qur’an 4:135 · Al-Nisa · Women
Stand firmly for justice
Believers are commanded to testify truthfully even against themselves or close relatives.
Focus while reading:
How does the verse address wealth, poverty, desire, and distortion?
4:135
O you who believe! Be you staunch in justice, witnesses for God, even though it be against yourselves or (your) parents or (your) kindred, whether (the case be of) a rich man or a poor man, for God is nearer to both (them you are). So follow not passion lest you lapse (from truth) and if you lapse or fall away, then indeed! God is ever Informed of what you do.

Qur’an 5:8 · Al-Ma’idah · The Table
Justice even toward those you dislike
Hostility does not cancel the obligation to be just.
Focus while reading:
What does the passage say about the moral danger of hatred?
5:8
O you who believe! Be steadfast witnesses for God in equity, and let not hatred of any people seduce you that you deal not justly. Deal justly, that is nearer to your duty. Observe your duty to God. Indeed, God is Informed of what you do.

## Supplement · Difficult Legal and Historical Passages

These readings are included because they remain important in debates about Islamic law, gender, slavery, sexuality, punishment, and Muhammad’s household. Historical explanation does not require moral approval, and modern Muslims interpret or apply these texts in different ways.

Qur’an 4:11–12 · Al-Nisa · Women
Inheritance shares
The verses guarantee women and other relatives specified shares while often assigning a male heir twice the share of a comparable female heir.
Focus while reading:
Map who receives shares and how family obligations shape the scheme.
4:11
God instructs you concerning your children: a male receives a share equal to that of two females. If there are more than two daughters, they receive two-thirds of the inheritance; if there is only one, she receives half. Each parent receives one-sixth if the deceased has a child. If there is no child and the parents alone inherit, the mother receives one-third; if there are siblings, the mother receives one-sixth. These distributions come after any bequest and payment of debt. You do not know whether your parents or your children are nearer to you in benefit. These are fixed shares from God. God is Knowing and Wise.
4:12
You receive half of what your wives leave if they have no child; if they have a child, you receive one-fourth, after any bequest and payment of debt. Wives receive one-fourth of what you leave if you have no child; if you have a child, they receive one-eighth, after any bequest and payment of debt. If a man or woman leaves neither parent nor child but has a maternal brother or sister, each receives one-sixth; if there are more, they share one-third, after any bequest and payment of debt, without harming the heirs. This is a command from God. God is Knowing and Forbearing.
Translation/context note:
The rules gave named property rights to women in their historical setting, but they are unequal by modern standards. Muslim-majority legal systems today vary in retaining, reforming, or supplementing them.

Qur’an 4:24 · Al-Nisa · Women
Marriage and those whom the right hand possesses
The verse prohibits married women as spouses except enslaved or captive women under the category ‘those whom your right hands possess.’
Focus while reading:
What forms of lawful and unlawful sexual relationship are distinguished?
4:24
Also forbidden to you are married women, except those whom your right hands possess. This is God's decree for you. All beyond these are lawful, provided that you seek them with your wealth in honorable marriage rather than sexual immorality. Give those whom you marry their required bridal gifts. There is no blame in whatever you mutually agree upon beyond the required gift. God is Knowing and Wise.
Translation/context note:
The phrase belongs to the institution of slavery and concubinage in late antiquity. Premodern Muslim law regulated rather than abolished that institution; modern Muslim states have legally abolished slavery, and most contemporary Muslims reject its revival.

Qur’an 2:282 · Al-Baqarah · The Cow
Debt contracts and witnesses
The Qur’an’s longest verse requires written debt contracts and witnesses.
Focus while reading:
What protections are established for debtor, creditor, scribe, and witnesses?
2:282
O you who believe! When you contract a debt for a fixed term, record it in writing. Let a scribe record it in writing between you in (terms of) equity. No scribe should refuse to write as God has taught him, so let him write, and let him who incurs the debt dictate, and let him observe his duty to God his Lord, and diminish nothing of it. But if he who owes the debt is of low understanding, or weak, or unable himself to dictate, then let the guardian of his interests dictate in (terms of) equity. And call to witness, from among your men, two witnesses. And if two men be not (at hand) then a man and two women, of such as you approve as witnesses, so that if the one errs (through forgetfulness) the other will remember. And the witnesses must not refuse when they are summoned. Be not averse to writing down (the contract) whether it be small or great, with (record of) the term of it. That is more equitable in the sight of God and more sure for testimony, and the best way of avoiding doubt between you; except only in the case when it is actual merchandise which you transfer among yourselves from hand to hand. In that case it is no sin for you if you write it not. And have witnesses when you sell one to another, and let no harm be done to scribe or witness. If you do (harm to them) indeed! it is a sin in you. Observe your duty to God. God is teaching you. And God is knower of all things.
Translation/context note:
The verse specifies two men, or one man and two women, for this financial transaction. Muslim jurists disagreed about whether and how this rule extends beyond debt contracts; modern interpreters also debate its social context and continuing application.

Qur’an 24:2 · Al-Nur · Light
Punishment for unlawful sexual intercourse
The verse prescribes one hundred lashes for zina, unlawful sexual intercourse.
Focus while reading:
What public and communal dimensions appear?
24:2
The woman and the man guilty of zina—unlawful sexual intercourse—lash each one of them one hundred lashes. Do not let pity for them keep you from carrying out God's judgment, if you believe in God and the Last Day; and let a group of believers witness their punishment.
Translation/context note:
The Qur’an does not prescribe stoning here. Premodern law developed very demanding evidentiary rules and, through hadith, additional penalties. Modern Muslim states differ sharply in legislation and enforcement.

Qur’an 33:50 · Al-Ahzab · The Confederates

Translation/context note:

Qur’an 65:4 · Al-Talaq · Divorce
Waiting periods and those who have not menstruated
The verse sets divorce waiting periods for postmenopausal women, pregnant women, and those who have not menstruated.
Focus while reading:
What categories and time periods are named?

Translation/context note:

## Translation and Source Note

The verse text is a PHIL 210 classroom adaptation of Mohammed Marmaduke Pickthall, The Meaning of the Glorious Koran (1930), a public-domain English translation. Archaic pronouns and verbs have been modernized, Allah is generally rendered “God,” and several disputed passages have been clarified through bracket-free classroom wording and explicit notes. Section framing is aligned with the course textbook’s treatment of the Qur’an, prophets, People of the Book, judgment, worship, ethics, gender, and jihad.
