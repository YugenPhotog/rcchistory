Historiography Frameworks

HIST 101 / 102 Reference Sheet
Purpose: A quick reference for the core “engines of change” used to interpret the past. Use this for discussions, quizzes, and essays.

1. Agency (Human Choice)

Core Idea: History is driven by human decision-making (choices, leadership, strategy).

Look For:
  • Individual leaders, pivotal turning points, and calculations.
  • Contingency: The idea that things could have gone differently.
Sentence Starters:
  • "A major turning point was when ___ decided to ___."
  • "This outcome depended on the choices of ___."
Example: Lincoln’s decisions during the Civil War.

2. Great Man Theory (Extreme Agency)

Core Idea: History is made primarily by exceptional individuals or heroic leaders.

Look For:
  • Hero-centered narratives; the "crowd" becomes background.
  • Terms like "Genius," "Destiny," or "Extraordinary Leadership".
Critique:

"If we remove the hero, what other forces still explain the change?"

Example: Napoleon as the sole explanation for European transformation.

3. Determinism (Structural Forces)

Core Idea: History is shaped by forces larger than individuals (Economics, Geography, Technology).

Look For:
  • Patterns across time; "Inevitable" language.
  • Structural pressures limiting individual options.
Sentence Starter:
  • "The larger system of ___ shaped outcomes more than any single person."
Example: Industrial capitalism shaping labor conditions.

4. Ideology (Belief Systems)

Core Idea: People act based on belief systems regarding truth, morality, and identity.

Look For:
  • Language of "Freedom," "Civilization," or "Tradition".
  • Rhetoric that divides groups into "In-groups" and "Out-groups".
Sentence Starter:
  • "The ideology of ___ shaped how people interpreted ___."
Example: Pro-slavery ideology and racial hierarchy in the 1800s.

5. Marxism (Class Conflict)

Core Idea: History is driven by material conditions and the struggle between owners and workers.

Look For:
  • Exploitation, inequality, and profit incentives.
  • Who benefits economically from the current system?
Sentence Starter:
  • "A Marxist interpretation would emphasize ___."
Example: Labor strikes (1877, 1894) interpreted through class conflict.

6. Nationalism (Identity & Loyalty)

Core Idea: People act in the name of national loyalty and shared identity.

Look For:
  • National myths, patriotic symbols, and "Unity" language.
  • Historical memory: Celebrating or silencing specific stories.
Example: WWI propaganda and national mobilization.

7. Evidence Check (Critical Reading)

Core Idea: An argument is only as strong as its evidence. Read like a historian.

Questions to Ask:
  • What is left out? Are statistics framed selectively?
  • Is rhetoric being used to push a moral conclusion?
Sentence Starter:
  • "The author supports this by citing ___, but does not address ___."
