ContextAbout This Source

The Book of Songs (Shijing) is one of the oldest surviving collections of Chinese poetry, gathering verses composed over several centuries in the early Zhou dynasty. The collection was later regarded as one of the Five Classics of the Confucian tradition and was studied and memorized for centuries as a foundation of literary and moral education.

The poem you are reading uses natural imagery and everyday scenes — funerals, quarrels, a shared meal — to reflect on family bonds, and in particular on the relationship between brothers. Read it closely and consider what it is arguing, and how it makes that argument, before turning to the questions below.
