Course Description

This course surveys the political, social, economic, and cultural development of the United States from pre-Columbian times through Reconstruction. We will explore the founding of the American colonies, the Revolutionary War, the formation of the Constitution, westward expansion, and the sectional conflicts that led to the Civil War and its aftermath.

Through primary source analysis, scholarly readings, and class discussions, students will develop critical thinking skills and gain a deeper understanding of the forces that shaped early American society. Special attention will be given to the experiences of diverse groups including Native Americans, African Americans, women, and immigrants.

Learning Objectives

1

Historical Analysis

Analyze primary and secondary sources to understand historical events and interpretations.

2

Critical Thinking

Evaluate competing historical narratives and form evidence-based arguments.

3

Historical Context

Connect past events to contemporary issues and understand historical continuity and change.

4

Diverse Perspectives

Examine American history through multiple lenses including race, class, gender, and region.
