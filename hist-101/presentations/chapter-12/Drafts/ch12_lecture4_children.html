The Children's Rights Movement

From the Moral Family to Civil Society:
How Reformers Reimagined Childhood in the American Republic

Chapter 12, Lecture 4 — HIST 101: American History to 1865

Why Children's Reform Matters

The Central Question

If self-government depends on virtue, how do we cultivate virtue in the next generation?

The Context

Antebellum Moral Reform

Expanded civic virtue beyond politics into moral life—temperance, asylums, education, and now children

Child Reform as Moral Project

Extended the moral reform project to the next generation—childhood as the foundation of democracy

Civil Society

Voluntary associations bridged private virtue and public welfare—citizens organizing to solve social problems

Scope of This Lecture

  • From Puritan discipline to Progressive child advocacy
  • From family authority to state protection
  • From moral reform to children's rights

The Children's Rights Movement didn't emerge suddenly—it grew from two centuries of evolving ideas about childhood, family, and democracy
