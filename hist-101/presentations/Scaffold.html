Lecture 1: Post-War of 1812 Political Events

National Republicans

  • By the end of the War of 1812, the Federalist Party was no longer a significant force in American Politics
  • Era of Good Feelings (one-party dominance)
  • Irony: Republicans adopt Federalist policies after Federalists disappear

📖 Understanding This Slide

Main idea: After the War of 1812, the Federalists fade, and Republicans briefly dominate—ironically adopting some Federalist-style policies during the Era of Good Feelings. Think of it like a team keeping its rival’s playbook because it works.

⚡ Content Breakdown

  • Federalists decline: By 1815, they’re no longer a major force.
  • Era of Good Feelings: One-party dominance means fewer open partisan fights, but tensions still simmer.
  • Policy irony: Republicans begin backing nation-building measures once linked to Federalists.

🏛️ Big Picture Synthesis

This slide sets up a political realignment: labels stay “Republican,” but the governing toolkit borrows from the old Federalist emphasis on national capacity. This means later debates won’t be about whether the nation should build; they’ll be about who has the power to do it and who benefits.

🎓 Historians Weigh In

Daniel Walker Howe, *What Hath God Wrought*

Howe emphasizes how national development and communications reshaped politics—clarifying why Republican adoption of “national” policies felt natural in this moment.
